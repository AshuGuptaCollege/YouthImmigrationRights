Mixed Status Households: In Public

Main Rights to Know in Public

  1. You do not have to consent to a search of your person
    Unless an ICE agent has a warrant signed by a judge or magistrate and you are not under arrest, you do not have to consent to a search.
  2. You can leave
    Unless the agent has a warrant signed by a judge or magistrate, and you are not being detained, you are free to leave.
  3. You do not have to justify speaking in any language
    If anyone - including an agent - asks you personal questions because of what language you are using, you are not required to answer them.
  4. You have a right to remain silent
    But remember, you cannot lie.
  5. You have a right to record video and photographs in public places (unless you interfere with immigration or legal enforcement).
