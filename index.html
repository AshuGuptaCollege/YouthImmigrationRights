Main Rights to Remember

  1. Your Right to Remain Silent
    You don’t have to answer questions from police or immigration. Ask if you are free to go.
  2. Your Right to Refuse Search
    You don’t have to let immigration search you or your home unless they have a warrant that has your name on it and is signed by a judge.
  3. Your Right to Get a Lawyer
    Don’t sign anything without a lawyer. The government will provide a lawyer at no cost if you are being charged with a crime. The government will not cover the cost of a lawyer for your immigration case.
  4. Your Right to Make a Phone Call
    Contact your lawyer or your family. You always have the right to contact your consulate. Immigration does not always give you access to a phone, but you should keep asking. Memorize your lawyer’s phone number.
  5. Your Right to Speak to a Judge Before Being Deported
    You usually will see a judge before being deported unless you are at the border.
  6. Your Right to Translation - Speak & Listen In Your Best Language
    The court will provide a translator in your best language.
  7. Your Right to Go to School
    Even if you are an undocumented child and have no immigration status, you still have a right to attend school while you are in the US.
