Detention & Deportation

FIVE THINGS TO DO

  1. Stay Silent
    You can refuse to discuss your immigration status when arrested and can refuse interviews with immigration agents while in police custody.
  2. Don’t Sign Anything Before Talking to a Lawyer
    You have a right to speak to a lawyer but not for free. You may receive a free lawyer if you’ve been arrested by police. You will need to provide your own lawyer if you’ve been detained by immigration.
  3. Don’t Run and Don’t Resist Arrest
    If you visit a friend in detention, you might be detained if you don’t have immigration status.
  4. Call Your Lawyer or Your Family
    You can contact your lawyer or your family. You should memorize at least one phone number.
  5. Talk to an Immigration Judge
    You will usually see a judge before being deported unless you are arrested or detained near the border. You have a right to a court interpreter in deportation proceedings. You cannot be deported if you are a US Citizen.
