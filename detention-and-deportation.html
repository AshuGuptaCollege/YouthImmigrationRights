Detention & Deportation

Main Rights to Know about Detention & Deportation

  1. You have a right to remain silent
    You can refuse to discuss your immigration status when detained and can refuse interviews with immigration agents while in police custody.
  2. Do not sign anything before speaking to a lawyer
    You have a right to speak to a lawyer. You may receive a free lawyer if you’ve been arrested by police. You will need to provide your own lawyer if you’ve been detained by immigration.
  3. You do not have a right to avoid capture or visit friends or family detained by immigration
    Don’t run and don’t resist arrest. If you visit detention, you might be detained if you don’t have immigration status.
  4. You usually have a right to make a phone call when detained
    You can contact your consulate, your lawyer, or your family.
  5. You have a right to see a judge before being deported, but there are exceptions at the border
    You have a right to a court interpreter in deportation proceedings. You cannot be deported if you are a US Citizen.
  6. The government should not treat you differently based on religion
    Sometimes the police will use things that take the place of religion to discriminate, but they are not supposed to treat you different because of your religion or race.
