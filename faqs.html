Frequently Asked Questions

What do I do if the police or immigration give me something to sign and tell me I will be in big trouble if I don’t sign it?

DO NOT SIGN IT. You are not required to sign anything the police or immigration give you before you have had an opportunity to speak with a lawyer. If you are arrested for a crime, you have a right to a lawyer provided by the government for free if you cannot afford your own. If you are detained by immigration, you have a right to a lawyer, but you must pay for the lawyer or find one who will volunteer to take your case.

If the police ask me about where I was born or whether I have legal immigration status, am I required to answer?

NO. You are not required to answer questions about where you were born. You have a right to remain silent.

Can I call people when I am detained?

Yes. You can contact your consulate, your lawyer, or your family.

What if I am not fluent in English, and I face deportation proceedings in a court?

You have a right to a court interpreter in deportation proceedings.

Will I be deported if I am a U.S. citizen?

No. If you are not a U.S. citizen, you can contact your consulate.

Can I go to school if I am an undocumented immigrant?

Yes, you can go to school. And you should!

Does my religion impact on my treatment as a undocumented immigrant, or my family?

NO. Sometimes the police will use things that take the place of religion to discriminate, but they are not supposed to treat you different because of your religion or race.

Can I take a video or take photographs of what’s happening between ICE and me on the street?

YES, as long as it does not interfere with immigration or legal enforcement.
