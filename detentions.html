Detentions

Your Rights With The Detention Process

  1. You have a right to remain silent (5th Amendment) → “Am I free to go?”
  2. You can refuse to discuss your immigration status when detained.
    • If in jail, you have a right to refuse interviews with immigration agents.
  3. Do not sign anything before speaking to a lawyer.
  4. You have a right to speak to a lawyer.
    • You may receive a free lawyer if you’ve been arrested by police.
    • You will need to provide your own lawyer if you’ve been detained by immigration.
    • If you don’t know, ask, “Are you the police?”
  5. You do not have a right to avoid capture or visit friends or family detained by immigration
    • Don’t run and don’t resist arrest.
    • If you visit detention, you might be detained if you don’t have immigration status.
  6. You usually have a right to make a phone call when detained.
  7. You cannot be deported if you are a US Citizen; if you aren’t, you can contact your consulate.
  8. You have a right to see a judge before deportation with exceptions (at border).
  9. You have a right to a court interpreter in deportation proceedings.
  10. The government is not supposed to treat you differently based on religion.
