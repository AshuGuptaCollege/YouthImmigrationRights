Immigration Encounters in Public Places

  1. You have a right to remain silent (5th A.) → “Am I free to go?”
  2. Don’t lie or mislead → you can be silent but don’t lie.
  3. Immigration officials should not stop or arrest you at schools, hospitals, places of worship, funerals, marches, rallies, and parades.
  4. You can refuse to answer questions about where you were born or are from.
  5. If you have immigration documents, the law requires you to carry them.
  6. You have a right to record video/photographs in public places (unless you interfere with immigration or legal enforcement).
